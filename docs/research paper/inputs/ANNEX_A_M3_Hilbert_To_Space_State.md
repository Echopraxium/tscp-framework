## A.1 Philosophical Foundation of State Emergence
In the TSCP Framework, the **M3 (Origin)** level does not define a static system but rather a field of potentials. The transition from pure abstraction to operational application is achieved through dimensionality reduction from the global Hilbert Space toward structured **State Spaces**.

## A.2 The Fundamental Hilbert Space $\mathcal{H}_{M3}$
The framework's origin is defined as a 4-dimensional complex Hilbert Space. 
* **Basis Vectors:** $\{ |S\rangle, |I\rangle, |D\rangle, |T\rangle \}$.
* **Property:** These vectors are mutually orthogonal (orthonormal basis), ensuring that no dimension can be reduced to or derived from another. This guarantees **Axiomatic Independence**.

## A.3 Projection Operators and State Spaces
The engine never operates on the entire Hilbert Space simultaneously; instead, it utilizes projection operators ($\hat{P}$) to generate two distinct, functionally specialized State Spaces.

### 1. Analytical State Space
Defined by the tensor product $\mathcal{T}_A = |S\rangle \otimes |I\rangle$.
This space is dedicated to **Knowledge and Coherence**. It stabilizes information within a defined structure. Any point within this space represents a "Systemic Snapshot" or a "Truth State." It answers the question: *What is the system's current form and content?*



### 2. Constructive State Space
Defined by the tensor product $\mathcal{T}_C = |D\rangle \otimes |T\rangle$.
This space is dedicated to **Will and Action**. It models the propulsion force of the system toward its finality (Teleonomy). Any point within this space represents an "Evolutionary Vector." It answers the question: *Where is the system going and how fast?*
