## IV. Layer M2: The Periodic Table of Systemic Elements

### 4.1 From Taxonomy to Combinatorial Chemistry
The transition from the M3 Hilbert Substrate to the M2 Layer represents the collapse of the wave function into specific "Systemic Isotopes." Just as the Standard Model of particle physics organizes the universe into quarks and leptons, TSCP organizes the complexity of systems into **50 Universal Invariants**.

This limitation is intentional. Adhering to the principle of **Parsimony (Occam's Razor)**, TSCP posits that the infinite variety of observable phenomena (M0) is generated by the combinatorial interaction of a finite set of fundamental elements (M2).

### 4.2 The Logic of Tensor Derivation
Each M2 Invariant is not merely a word; it is a fixed vector $|\psi_{inv}\rangle$ derived from the M3 basis. The derivation follows the canonical projection formula:

$$|\text{Invariant}\rangle = c_S|S\rangle + c_I|I\rangle + c_D|D\rangle + c_T|T\rangle$$

Where the coefficients $c$ represent the "amplitude" of each dimension in the invariant's nature. This allows us to calculate the **Semantic Distance** between concepts.

### 4.3 Detailed Analysis of the 8 Functional Families

We categorize the 50 invariants into 8 families based on their vector dominance. Below are the "Technical Sheets" for the cardinal invariants of each family.

#### FAMILY A: ARCHITECTURE (Dominance: $|S\rangle$)
*Focus: Topology, Containment, Spatial Organization.*

**Cardinal Invariant: `m2:Boundary`**
* **Tensor Signature:** `[ S:0.8 | I:0.1 | D:0.0 | T:0.1 ]`
* **Definition:** The topological limit separating the internal System from the external Environment. It is the filter of entropy.
* **Derivation Logic:** A boundary is almost purely structural ($|S\rangle$). However, it must contain a trace of Information ($|I\rangle$) to distinguish "Self" from "Non-Self" (selectivity) and a trace of Teleonomy ($|T\rangle$) to serve the purpose of protection.
* **Cross-Domain Isomorphism:**
    * **Biology:** The Phospholipid Bilayer (Cell Membrane). It physically blocks molecules ($S$) but contains protein channels ($I$) to allow nutrients.
    * **Cybernetics:** The Firewall. It defines the trusted network perimeter.
    * **Geopolitics:** The Border. A line defining sovereignty.

**Other Family Members:** `Substrate`, `Layer`, `Interface`, `Component`.

---

#### FAMILY B: PHYSICS (Dominance: $|D\rangle + |S\rangle$)
*Focus: Energy, Resources, Thermodynamics.*

**Cardinal Invariant: `m2:Resource`**
* **Tensor Signature:** `[ S:0.6 | I:0.0 | D:0.4 | T:0.0 ]`
* **Definition:** A finite potential stored within a structure, capable of being converted into work.
* **Derivation Logic:** A resource is "frozen dynamics" ($|D\rangle$) held within a physical container ($|S\rangle$). It has zero Teleonomy until an Agent utilizes it.
* **Cross-Domain Isomorphism:**
    * **Finance:** Liquidity (Cash reserves).
    * **Biology:** ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate).
    * **Computing:** RAM / CPU Cycles.

**Other Family Members:** `Energy`, `Entropy`, `Matter`, `Waste`.

#### FAMILY C: DYNAMICS (Dominance: $|D\rangle$)
*Focus: Time, Transition, State Change.*

**Cardinal Invariant: `m2:Gradient`**
* **Tensor Signature:** `[ S:0.3 | I:0.0 | D:0.7 | T:0.0 ]`
* **Definition:** A differential in potential between two points in the State Space that induces Flow.
* **The Cyclist Metaphor:** To move, a cyclist needs a slope.
    * If Gradient = 0, the system is in **Stasis** (Entropy maximizes).
    * If Gradient > Threshold, the system suffers **Rupture**.
* **Systemic Function:** The Gradient is the "Engine" of the system. Without a difference (in voltage, price, or concentration), there is no life.

**Other Family Members:** `Flow`, `Event`, `Threshold`, `Mode`, `Cycle`.

#### FAMILY D: AGENCY (Balanced Vectors)
*Focus: Autonomy, Action, Perception.*

**Cardinal Invariant: `m2:Agent`**
* **Tensor Signature:** `[ S:0.3 | I:0.2 | D:0.3 | T:0.2 ]`
* **Definition:** An entity capable of closing the loop between Perception ($|I\rangle$), Processing, and Action ($|D\rangle$).
* **Derivation Logic:** The Agent is the "Golden Ratio" of systems. It needs a body ($S$) to exist, senses ($I$) to perceive, muscles ($D$) to act, and a will ($T$) to persist.
* **Cross-Domain Isomorphism:**
    * **Biology:** The Mitochondria (before endosymbiosis).
    * **Economics:** The "Homo Economicus" or the Market Maker.
    * **Software:** The Daemon or Microservice.

**Other Family Members:** `Collective`, `Proxy`, `Role`.

#### FAMILY E: SEMANTICS (Dominance: $|I\rangle$)
*Focus: Meaning, Encoding, Transmission.*

**Cardinal Invariant: `m2:Code`**
* **Tensor Signature:** `[ S:0.1 | I:0.9 | D:0.0 | T:0.0 ]`
* **Definition:** A set of symbols and syntax rules used to store Information independent of the Dynamics.
* **Derivation Logic:** Code aims to be pure Information. The minimal Structure ($S:0.1$) is the physical medium (ink, silicon charge) required to store it, but the value is entirely in the $I$ vector.
* **Cross-Domain Isomorphism:**
    * **Genetics:** DNA Codons (A, C, T, G).
    * **IT:** Binary (0, 1).
    * **Law:** The Constitution (Written text).

**Other Family Members:** `Language`, `Signal`, `Message`, `Protocol`.

#### FAMILY F: CONTROL (Dominance: $|I\rangle + |T\rangle$)
*Focus: Regulation, Feedback, Correction.*

**Cardinal Invariant: `m2:Rule`**
* **Tensor Signature:** `[ S:0.0 | I:0.6 | D:0.0 | T:0.4 ]`
* **Definition:** An informational constraint imposed on the system to align Dynamics with Teleonomy.
* **Derivation Logic:** A Rule has no mass ($S=0$). It is Information ($I$) serving a Purpose ($T$).
* **The GameStop Failure:** The "Rule" (Short Selling Regulation) failed because it had no $S$ (Physical enforcement) to stop the $D$ (Market volume).
* **Systemic Function:** To reduce the variety of the system (Ashby's Law) to manageable levels.

**Other Family Members:** `Feedback`, `Regulation`, `Constraint`, `Standard`.

#### FAMILY G: TELEONOMY (Dominance: $|T\rangle$)
